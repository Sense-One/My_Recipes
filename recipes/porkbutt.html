Smoked Pork Butt

This recipe is for a delicious juicy smoked pork shoulder. By the time this is done cooking, the bone will pull right out and the meat will fall apart. This will be cooked in an offset smoker with mesquite wood and cherry wood chips.

This will be cooked between the temperatures of 220-250 degrees and can take anywhere from 8-12 hours, depending on the size of the shoulder. During the cooking time, every hour the meat will be spritzed with a mixture of apple juice and apple cider vinegar.

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons Salt
  • 3½ tablespoons Sugar
  • 1¼ tablespoons Garlic Powder
  • 1¼ tablespoons Paprika
  • 1 tablespoons Chili Powder
  • ¾ tablespoons Celery Salt
  • ¾ tablespoons Lemon Pepper
  • ½ tablespoons Pepper
  • ¼ tablespoons White Pepper
  • ¼ tablespoons Red Pepper

Steps

  1. Pre-heat grill.
  2. Mix all seasonings into a bowl.
  3. Trim extra fat off meat and rub down with yellow mustard.
  4. Rub pre-made seasoning onto the the pork shoulder.
  5. Once the grill has reached a tempature between 220-250 degrees put shoulder at the furthest end.
  6. Check meat every hour to spritz with apple juice/apple cider vinegar.
  7. Once internal tempature has reached 160-165 degrees wrap the shoulder in foil and continue smoking until the tempature is 195-205 degrees.
